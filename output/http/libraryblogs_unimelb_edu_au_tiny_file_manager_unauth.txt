	<h1 class="entry-header"><a href="https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/2024/04/16/rafu-shimpo-digital-archive-trial/" rel="bookmark">Rafu Shimpo Digital Archive (TRIAL)</a></h1>
		<p>Access via <a href="https://cat.lib.unimelb.edu.au:443/record=e1002352~S31">Library Catalogue</a>.<br />
Trial ends: <strong>16th May 2024</strong>.<br />
<b>Archive coverage: 1914-1942, 1946-current</b><br />
The Rafu Shimpo (羅府新報, L.A. Japanese Daily News) is the longest-running Japanese American newspaper in the United States. The paper began in 1903 supporting the small but growing Japanese community in the Little Tokyo area of Los Angeles, California. By the 1940s it was the most widely circulated paper in the region and included a weekly English section for second generation Japanese Americans. With the onset of World War II, the paper was forced to cease publication after its publisher, H. Toyosaku Komai, and most of the Komai family were arrested and interned for the duration of the war. The Rafu Shimpo quickly revived publication after the war and capitalized on being the first Japanese American newspaper to resume publication in January of 1946. The newspaper outlasted all its local competitors and grew to become the most prominent and preeminent Japanese American publication in the United States.</p>

	<h1 class="entry-header"><a href="https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/2024/02/02/british-online-archives-censorship-policy-and-practice-during-the-second-world-war/" rel="bookmark">British Online Archives &#8211; Censorship: Policy and Practice During the Second World War</a></h1>
		<p><a href="https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/files/2024/02/BOA_Censorship-policy-and-practice-during-the-second-world-war-cb0aa0f57c381bbf.PNG" data-build-figure="full-width"><img decoding="async" class="wp-image-5404 alignleft" src="https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/files/2024/02/BOA_Censorship-policy-and-practice-during-the-second-world-war-cb0aa0f57c381bbf-300x214.png" alt="" width="300" height="214" srcset="https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/files/2024/02/BOA_Censorship-policy-and-practice-during-the-second-world-war-cb0aa0f57c381bbf-300x214.png 300w, https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/files/2024/02/BOA_Censorship-policy-and-practice-during-the-second-world-war-cb0aa0f57c381bbf-1024x731.png 1024w, https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/files/2024/02/BOA_Censorship-policy-and-practice-during-the-second-world-war-cb0aa0f57c381bbf-768x549.png 768w, https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/files/2024/02/BOA_Censorship-policy-and-practice-during-the-second-world-war-cb0aa0f57c381bbf-600x429.png 600w, https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/files/2024/02/BOA_Censorship-policy-and-practice-during-the-second-world-war-cb0aa0f57c381bbf.PNG 1134w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>Access via <a href="https://cat.lib.unimelb.edu.au:443/record=e1002334~S31">Library Catalogue</a>.</p>
<p><b>Censorship: Practice and Policy during the Second World War</b> explores British postal and telegraph censorship throughout a pivotal era of modern history. The collection contains images drawn from Ministry of Defence files at The National Archives. It begins by surveying the first tentative steps that were taken, following the outbreak of conflict in 1939, to implement and to co-ordinate censorship. Documents reveal how hastily-assembled censorship teams looked back to the First World War for lessons and instructional precedents whilst adapting to the major technological developments that had occurred since. The work undertaken by censorship units — which necessitated keeping a close eye on all communications that could reveal sensitive or dangerous information to the enemy — soon became one of the most important, if somewhat underappreciated, fronts in the so-called “secret war”. Censorship became a wide-ranging, international endeavour. It involved not just Britain, but also the Dominions, colonies, allies (including the USA), and neutral states such as Ireland. Censorship teams sprung-up across the globe, typically employing local people, especially women. Censors from a wide variety of backgrounds became adept at breaking codes and recognising suspect material, even if it was cleverly concealed.</p>

<b>Archive coverage: 1872-1949</b><br />
Established in 1872, Shen Bao (申報, historically transliterated as Shun Pao or Shen-pao) was the most influential and longest lasting commercial newspaper before the establishment of the People’s Republic. Published in Shanghai until 1949, Shen Bao was founded by Englishman Ernest Major, but, uniquely, as a newspaper for Chinese readers, written by Chinese reporters. During its existence, Shen Bao gradually shifted from a conservative to a more liberal perspective, and played a pivotal role in the formation of public opinion in the imperial period and into the tumultuous beginnings of modern China. Shen Bao’s innovations in printing technologies, specialized use of the telegraph, and dispatch of special military correspondents gave it an edge.</p>

		<p><a href="https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/files/2023/09/RRIMO.png" data-build-figure="full-width"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="wp-image-5343 alignleft" src="https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/files/2023/09/RRIMO.png" alt="" width="264" height="101" /></a>Access via <a href="https://cat2.lib.unimelb.edu.au:443/record=e1002304~S31">Library Catalogue</a>.<br />
Trial ends: <strong>10th November 2023</strong>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>RRIMO</b> provides online access to the seven ongoing Recent Researches in Music series: Medieval, Renaissance, Baroque, Classical, 19th/20th centuries, American (including MUSA), and Oral Traditions. Titles in the series Collegium Musicum: Yale University are also included in <b>RRIMO</b>.</p>

	<h1 class="entry-header"><a href="https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/2023/09/06/wiley-digital-archives-environmental-science-and-history-trial/" rel="bookmark">Wiley Digital Archives: Environmental Science and History &#8211; (TRIAL)</a></h1>
		<p>Access via <a href="https://cat2.lib.unimelb.edu.au:443/record=e1002301~S31">Library Catalogue</a>.<br />
Trial ends: <strong>28th October 2023</strong>.</p>
<p><strong>Please Note:</strong> Access to this trial is via the same landing page as our access to Wiley&#8217;s <em><strong>Royal Geographical Society collection</strong></em>. The trial is only to Wiley&#8217;s <em><strong>Environmental Science &amp; History Collection</strong></em>.</p>
<p><em><strong>Wiley Digital Archives: Environmental Science and History</strong></em> focuses on critical aspects of anthropogenic change, with unique and rare archival collections from multiple, global sources.</p>
<p>This archive includes collections from the following primal sources:</p>
<ul>
<li>Royal Botanic Gardens,</li>
<li>The National Archives (UK),</li>
<li>The Commonwealth Forestry Institute,</li>
<li>CAB International (CABI),</li>
<li>Royal Entomological Society,</li>
<li>Ecological Society of America.</li>
</ul>
<p>The collection contains approximately one million pages/images of primary sources, derived from new scanning and digitization, featuring data-heavy collections on Deforestation, Agriculture, Livestock, and Fisheries (Food Production); Ecology, Botany, Biodiversity, and Extinction; Insect Science; Water Sources, Irrigation, Wetlands, and Hydrology.</p>
<p>Like this database? Please leave a <a href="https://libraryblogs.unimelb.edu.au/2023/08/23/wiley-digital-archives-environmental-science-and-history-trial/">comment.</a></p>
